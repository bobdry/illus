5: Doom Slide

They must work quickly.

“Why is your father taking so loooong out there? And Lucy, why are you unbuckled?”

One squirrel directs the efforts from atop Wilson.

“I’ve got to change out of these clothes mom, they’re disgusting. I’m getting my bag.”

Boots are secured with a well designed rig of vines.

“Oh it ain’t that bad, big sis, just a little soggy.”

A pathway is iced.

“Quiet kids. Uh, yes, is this Ralph’s towing service? Oh great, we are in some big trouble. We slid off the road on OH-800 about two miles from exit 202 off of I-70.”

The descent begins.

With great care, the squirrels slide Wilson into the woods.

The mammalian caravan travels far from the roadside.

Around a curve there’s a large pile of enormous rocks. Nestled at the center is the squirrel’s secret destination.

Wilson’s crossed arms and chest are too much to fit through the cave entrance. They’ll need to be untied and maneuvered just right.

Success, the squirrels twist him into the hole.

One of the dogs, a pit bull named Rosie, stays back for one last check of the surroundings before entering the cave.
