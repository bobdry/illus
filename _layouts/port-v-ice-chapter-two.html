2: Aggression

“Why so many kids?”

“Uh, well, you know. We love our children and wanted to have more.”

“Yeah, but, a lot of mouths to feed. You couldn’t settle for just two or one or maybe even none? ”

“Children are a gift from God, genius. We’re thankful to have ’em.”

“Ho ho ho, easy now, buddy.”

“Same to you, pal.”

Luckily, at that moment, Monica emerges from the bathroom to diffuse the bomb that’s about to go off.

“Thank you sir, I needed that big time.”

“Yes ma’am.”

Wilson completes the purchase and they all exit the station.

At the pump, Wilson fills the tank and mulls the interaction with the man in the gas station.

I bet he’s leering at us through the window right now, Wilson thinks.

He worries about his kids while Max worries about his potato chips.

“Everyone ready to get out of here and back home?” Wilson asks. The kids respond, “Yeah Dad, let’s get outta here!”

“Thanks, honey, for pulling over.” Monica smiles, detecting his frustration.

It seems like maybe there was something going on in the gas station. Or maybe just having to stop in this nasty weather is bothering him?

As they head back to the main highway, ice and sleet have picked up again.

Wilson adjusts his speed down, determined to push through the storm.
