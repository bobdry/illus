2: Meanness

“Why so many kids?”

“Uh, well, you know. We love our children and wanted to have more.”

“Yeah, but, a lot of mouths to feed. You couldn’t settle for just two or one or maybe even none? ”

“My family is none of your business. We’ll settle things here and be on our way real soon, pal.”

“That will be just fine, buddy. Careful out there though, roads are looking bad.”

“We’ll manage.”

Monica emerges from the bathroom, feeling much better.

“Thank you sir, I needed that big time.”

“Yes ma’am.”

Wilson completes the purchase and they all exit the station.

At the pump, Wilson fills the tank and mulls the station interaction.

He thinks, I bet that dude is leering at us through the window right now.

But I don’t care. Lucy and Max are amazing and their little brother will be too.

“Everyone ready to get out of here and back home?” he asks. The kids respond, “Yeah Dad, let’s get outta here!”

“Thanks again for stopping, honey.” Monica can tell something’s bothering Wilson.

Maybe it’s just the bad weather? But what was going on between him and that clerk?

As they head back to the main highway, ice and sleet have really picked up again.

Wilson adjusts his speed down, determined to push through the storm.
