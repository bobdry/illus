3: Wreck

fooosh

The conditions get even worse. He swears the road wasn’t this curvy on the way in.

thunk thunk thunk thunk thunk thunk

He shouldn’t be doing this. He can barely see the road. Woah! That curve is way too tight!

kooosh

Total loss of traction.

Total loss of control.

And they’re in the ditch.

Wilson panics, “Is everyone ok? Monica are you ok? Lucy?!? Max ?!?”

“Yes, I think  we're ok.”

“Yeah dad, we’re ok, other than the choco milk and pop bath we just got. Gross.”

“This weather is horrible. There was nothing like this in the forecast when we left your parent’s house. I feel awful dragging us out here for a potty break. I’m calling Triple A.”

“Now just wait a minute, let me get out and see what the situation is. Maybe we can get back on the road.”

“Just give me a chance to look.”

Out of the van, Wilson can see that they are indeed stuck. The left front tire is badly bent up and under the van.

The ice and sleet ease up.

They must of skidded more than 100 feet into the ditch.

There’s probably a half inch of ice accumulated on the asphalt.

It’s cold. Really cold. And Monica, as usual, knows best. They’ll have to call Triple A to get them out of this mess.

He turns to head back to the van.

Easy now.

Trying to stabilize.

And failing.

ka crunch crack

“Ooo, my back.” Wilson exhales a little pain and a lot of frustration.
